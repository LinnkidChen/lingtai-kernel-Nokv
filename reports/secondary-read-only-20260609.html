Secondary read-only boundary

Scope: kernel secondary onlyNormal primary send/reply unchangedDo not merge without approval

Conclusion

This change keeps the existing reserved nested secondary architecture, but constrains it to read-only fetches. Nested secondary.send and secondary.reply are no longer advertised by schemas or guidance, and runtime validation rejects them before dispatch.

Why

Jason chose the read-only-only direction after a real Telegram incident showed that nested human-facing communication side effects are too easy for current models to misuse under pressure. The safe remaining use case is fetching the full body of a just-notified message before a long primary tool starts.
